Yes — on the Cambridge English Dictionary homepage you can choose which dictionary you want to search in. Here’s how:

---

## 👀 Visual Instructions
1. Look at the **top of the page header**, above or beside the search bar.  
2. You’ll see a **horizontal menu** with options such as:  
   - **English**  
   - **Learner’s Dictionary**  
   - **Essential British English**  
   - **Essential American English**  
3. Click on the dictionary you want to use.  
   - For example, if you click **Learner’s Dictionary**, the search bar will then search within that dictionary.  
4. Once selected, type your word into the search bar and press **Enter**.  
   - The results will come from the dictionary you chose.

---

## 🎧 Screen Reader Instructions
1. From the top of the page, use **Tab** or arrow keys to move through the **header menu links**.  
2. Your screen reader will announce each option as a **link**:  
   - *“Link: English”*  
   - *“Link: Learner’s Dictionary”*  
   - *“Link: Essential British English”*  
   - *“Link: Essential American English”*  
3. When you hear the dictionary you want, press **Enter**.  
   - This switches the search context to that dictionary.  
4. Navigate to the **search bar** (announced as *“Search English, edit text”* or similar).  
5. Type your word and press **Enter**.  
   - The definition will load from the selected dictionary.

---

✅ So: you select the dictionary by clicking (or activating with your screen reader) one of the **menu links in the header**, then use the search bar.
