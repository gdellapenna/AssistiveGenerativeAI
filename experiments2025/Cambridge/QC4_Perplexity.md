To search for a specific word on the Cambridge English Dictionary page:

### Visual Instructions:
- Find the search box located at the top of the page. It is a rectangular text input field labeled “Search the Cambridge Dictionary” or similar.
- Click into the search box to place your cursor there.
- Type the word you want to look up.
- Press the Enter key on your keyboard or click the search icon (usually a magnifying glass) next to the box.
- The page will refresh with the results showing the dictionary entry for the word you typed.

### Screen Reader Instructions:
- Upon page load, find the search form landmark or the input field labeled with “Search the Cambridge Dictionary.”
- Use your screen reader’s command to jump to form controls or the search box.
- When you hear the label “Search the Cambridge Dictionary,” start typing the word you want to find.
- After typing the word, press the Enter key.
- The screen reader will then announce the dictionary entry page with the searched word as the main heading.
- Navigate through the headings and regions on that page to explore the word’s meaning, examples, usage notes, and pronunciation options.

This search function supports quick lookup of any English word or phrase you want on the Cambridge Dictionary website.[2][3][5]
