Yes — on the Cambridge English Dictionary homepage you can switch to a **bilingual dictionary** (for example English–Spanish, English–French, English–German, etc.). Here’s how:

---

## 🖥️ Visual Directions (sighted navigation)

1. At the very **top of the page**, look just above the main search bar.  
   - You’ll see a small menu option that currently says something like **“English (UK)”** or **“English (US)”**.
2. Click on this option.  
   - A drop‑down menu will appear showing different dictionary choices.
3. In that menu, you’ll find **bilingual dictionaries** listed (e.g., *English–Spanish*, *English–French*, *English–German*).
4. Click on the bilingual dictionary you want.  
   - The page will reload, and the search bar will now use that bilingual dictionary.

---

## 🎧 Screen Reader Directions

1. From the very top of the page, press **Tab** until your screen reader announces something like **“English (UK)”** or **“English (US)”**.  
   - It will be announced as a **link** or **menu button**.
2. Press **Enter** to activate it.  
   - A list of dictionary options will open.
3. Use the **Arrow keys** to move through the list.  
   - You’ll hear options such as “English–Spanish,” “English–French,” “English–German.”
4. Press **Enter** on the bilingual dictionary you want.  
   - The page reloads, and the search bar is now set to that bilingual dictionary.

---

✅ In short: yes, you can switch to a bilingual dictionary using the **dictionary selector at the top of the page**.
