The Cambridge English Dictionary website, located at dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english, is designed to be accessible and usable for people with disabilities, including those using screen readers. 

### Visual Navigation Summary:
- At the top of the page, you will usually find a search bar where you can type the word you want to look up.
- There are likely navigation menus or links to sections such as "English Dictionary," "Thesaurus," and language learning resources.
- Definitions for searched words appear in the main content area, with clear headings and pronunciation audio links.
- There may be options to hear pronunciations in British and American English.
- Accessibility features probably include text-to-speech and font size adjustments, though these might be part of an accessibility toolbar you can activate.

### Screen Reader Instructions Summary:
- When the page loads, the screen reader focuses on the main content area with a search input labeled clearly such as “Search the Cambridge Dictionary.”
- Navigate using the standard screen reader commands to jump to the search form (usually a landmark or form role).
- Enter the word you want to look up and activate the search by pressing the Enter key.
- The results page will present a heading with the searched word, followed by its definitions announced as separate regions or headings.
- Pronunciation buttons can be found near the word’s heading; these are labeled with their accent (e.g., British English pronunciation) and can be activated with the Enter or Space key to hear the audio.
- The site supports screen reader-friendly navigation with headings and landmarks for easy scanning.
- Accessibility tools like text-to-speech or font size adjustments, if available, can be activated by navigating to the toolbar at the top of the page (often labeled “Accessibility Tools”).

This summary is based on the Cambridge Dictionary site accessibility principles and common interface elements described in their accessibility statements. Specific on-page interaction steps depend on the exact layout and current page content, but the above instructions should guide you efficiently on the English dictionary main page[1][4]
