Yes — the Cambridge Dictionary homepage does allow you to **choose which dictionary** you want to search in. Here’s how it works:

---

## 🖥️ Visual Directions (sighted navigation)

1. At the very **top of the page**, just above the main search bar, look for a small **drop‑down menu** or text link next to the Cambridge logo.  
   - It usually shows **“English (UK)”** or **“English (US)”**.
2. Click on this option.  
   - A menu will open, letting you select different dictionaries (for example: English–English, English–Spanish, English–French, etc.).
3. Once you select a dictionary, the search bar will use that dictionary for your queries.  
   - The label above the search bar will update to show which dictionary is active.

---

## 🎧 Screen Reader Directions

1. From the very top of the page, press **Tab** until you hear something like **“English (UK)”** or **“English (US)”** announced as a **link** or **menu button**.
2. Activate it with **Enter**.  
   - Your screen reader will announce a list of dictionary options.
3. Use the **Arrow keys** to move through the list (e.g., “English–Spanish,” “English–French,” “English–English”).
4. Press **Enter** on the dictionary you want.  
   - The page will reload, and the search bar will now use that dictionary.

---

✅ In short: yes, you can select the dictionary by using the **language/dictionary selector at the top of the page**, right above the search bar.
