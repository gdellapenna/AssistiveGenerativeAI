The structure of the Cambridge English Dictionary web page generally includes several key sections, based on typical HTML layout and content organization:

### Visual Structure:
- **Header**: At the top, there is usually a header section that contains the website logo, the main navigation menu, and the search box for entering words.
- **Search Box**: Prominently placed near the top, it allows users to input words they want to look up. It often includes a label like “Search the Cambridge Dictionary.”
- **Main Content Area**: Directly below or beside the search box, this area displays the search results or dictionary entry for a specific word.
- **Definitions Section**: This part shows the main entries, including pronunciation guides with audio links, part of speech, and detailed definitions.
- **Additional Links or Buttons**: Often, links to related words, synonyms, or additional resources are available.
- **Footer**: At the bottom, there are typically links to terms of use, privacy policy, and contact information.

### HTML and Structural Elements:
- **Header Tags (h1, h2, etc.)**: Used for titles, the searched word, and section headings.
- **Forms**: The search input is within a `<form>` element, allowing for input submission.
- **Input Elements**: The search box itself is an `<input>` element, with type ‘search’ or ‘text’.
- **Navigation Lists**: Navigation menus are often within `<nav>` or `<ul>` elements, containing links to other pages or sections.
- **Content Divs and Sections**: The definitions and related content are formatted within `<div>` or `<section>` tags, which are styled with CSS.
- **Audio Links**: Pronunciation audios are embedded as `<audio>` elements or linked via `<a>` tags pointing to MP3 files.

### Accessibility Elements:
- The page includes landmarks like `<main>`, `<header>`, and `<footer>` to assist screen reader navigation.
- Labels for form controls (like the search box) are clearly associated with `<label>` tags.
- Headings are structured sequentially for easy navigation.
