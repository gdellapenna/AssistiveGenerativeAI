Yes — the Cambridge English Dictionary homepage does include **menus**. Let me break them down clearly:

---

## 🖥️ Visual Description of Menus

- **Top Header Menu (very top of the page):**
  - On the right side of the header, you’ll see small text links that act as a menu:
    - **English** (language selector — lets you change site language)
    - **+Plus** (Cambridge Dictionary Plus subscription area)
    - **Login** (sign in to your account)
    - **English (UK)** (toggle between UK/US dictionary versions)

- **Main Navigation Menu (below the search bar):**
  - This is a horizontal menu with options such as:
    - **Dictionary**
    - **Translate**
    - **Grammar**
    - **Thesaurus**
    - **+Plus**
    - **Cambridge Dictionary Labs**
  - Each item is clickable and takes you to a different section of the site.

- **Footer Menu (bottom of the page):**
  - Contains links grouped together:
    - **About Cambridge Dictionary**
    - **Privacy Policy**
    - **Help**
    - **Contact Us**
    - Social media icons (Facebook, Twitter, YouTube)

---

## 🎧 Screen Reader–Friendly Menu Structure

- **Header Menu:**
  - Navigate to the top of the page.
  - Use **Tab** to move through links: “English,” “+Plus,” “Login,” “English (UK).”
  - Screen reader will announce each as a **link**.

- **Main Navigation Menu:**
  - After the search bar, press **Tab** or use **Link List (Insert+F7 in JAWS/NVDA)**.
  - You’ll hear links: “Dictionary,” “Translate,” “Grammar,” “Thesaurus,” “+Plus,” “Cambridge Dictionary Labs.”
  - Activate any with **Enter**.

- **Footer Menu:**
  - Scroll to the bottom or use **H** to jump to headings near the footer.
  - Links announced as: “About Cambridge Dictionary,” “Privacy Policy,” “Help,” “Contact Us.”
  - Social media icons are also links, announced as “link, Facebook,” “link, Twitter,” etc.

---
