Yes — the Cambridge English Dictionary homepage does include **menu options** in its header and footer. Let me describe them clearly for you:

---

## 👀 Visual Menu Layout

### 1. **Header Menu (Top of Page)**
- Located at the very top, above or beside the search bar.  
- Options include:
  - **English** – the main dictionary.  
  - **Learner’s Dictionary** – simplified definitions for learners.  
  - **Essential British English** – dictionary focused on British usage.  
  - **Essential American English** – dictionary focused on American usage.  

These appear as clickable links in a horizontal row.

---

### 2. **Footer Menu (Bottom of Page)**
- At the very bottom of the page, there is a set of smaller links.  
- Options typically include:
  - **About Cambridge Dictionary**  
  - **Privacy Policy**  
  - **Terms of Use**  
  - **Help**  
  - **Contact Us**  

These are text links arranged in a horizontal or vertical list.

---

## 🎧 Screen Reader Menu Navigation

### 1. **Header Menu**
- Use **Tab** from the top of the page.  
- Screen reader will announce each option as a **link**:  
  - *“Link: English”*  
  - *“Link: Learner’s Dictionary”*  
  - *“Link: Essential British English”*  
  - *“Link: Essential American English”*  
- Press **Enter** on any of these to load that dictionary version.

### 2. **Footer Menu**
- Navigate to the **footer landmark** (often announced as *“Content info”*).  
- Screen reader will read each link:  
  - *“Link: About Cambridge Dictionary”*  
  - *“Link: Privacy Policy”*  
  - *“Link: Terms of Use”*  
  - *“Link: Help”*  
  - *“Link: Contact Us”*  
- Press **Enter** to activate the desired option.

---
