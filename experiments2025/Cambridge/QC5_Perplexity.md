On the Cambridge English Dictionary page, the search bar is located at the top of the page, clearly labeled as the main input area for searching words. 

### Visual Instructions:
- The search bar is a rectangular text input field, placed near the upper center or upper right portion of the page.
- It comes with a visible label such as “Search the Cambridge Dictionary” or simply “Search.”
- Next to this input box, there is usually a magnifying glass icon which acts as the search button.
- To use it, click inside the box, type your desired word, and press Enter or click the magnifying glass icon to start the search.

### Screen Reader Instructions:
- When the page loads, the screen reader typically moves the focus or can be directed to the search region with the form landmark.
- Listen for the label announced as “Search the Cambridge Dictionary” or “Search” linked to a text input field.
- This input field is where you type the word you want to find.
- After typing, press Enter on the keyboard.
- The screen reader will then announce the new page or area with the dictionary entry for the searched word.
- The search field is the central and first interactive element for navigation on this page, making it easy to reach with quick landmark or form navigation commands.

This placement is consistent for all main pages on the Cambridge Dictionary website, ensuring easy access to search functionality.[1][4][5]
