Yes, you absolutely can switch to a bilingual dictionary on this page. The page contains a large section dedicated to various translation dictionaries.

### Switching to a Bilingual Dictionary

**👁️ Visual Directions:**

1.  Scroll down the page past the main search, the single-language dictionary options, and the Grammar and Thesaurus sections.
2.  Look for the main heading or section titled **Translation**.
3.  Under this section, you will see a list of links organized into two subsections:
    * **Bilingual Dictionaries:** These are listed with two-way options (e.g., **English–Spanish** and **Spanish–English**).
    * **Semi-bilingual Dictionaries:** These are listed with one-way options (e.g., **English–Arabic**, **English–Vietnamese**).
4.  Click on the link for the language pair you wish to use (e.g., **English–German**).

**🔊 Screen Reader Directions:**

1.  Use link navigation or heading navigation to find the section related to **Translations** or **Bilingual Dictionaries**.
2.  Look for and activate the link corresponding to your desired language pair. For example, if you want to switch to the Spanish dictionary, look for the link **English–Spanish** or **Spanish–English**.
3.  The bilingual dictionary links include options for Dutch, French, German, Italian, Spanish, Portuguese, Chinese, and many more.
