Yes, you can switch to a bilingual dictionary on the Cambridge English Dictionary page.

### How to switch to a bilingual dictionary:
- Near the search box or at the top of the page, there is an option or dropdown menu to select from multiple bilingual dictionaries.
- Cambridge Dictionary offers around 22 bilingual dictionaries, including English to Spanish, English to French, English to Chinese (Simplified and Traditional), English to German, English to Italian, English to Japanese, and many more language pairs.
- By selecting a bilingual dictionary option from this menu, your searches will look up words with translations and definitions in both English and the selected language.
- This option typically appears as a language or dictionary selector that you can open and navigate through.
- Once selected, you enter the word in the search box as usual, and the results provide bilingual definitions and translations.

### Screen reader guidance:
- Use quick navigation to locate a dropdown or combobox labeled as “Select Dictionary,” “Translation Dictionaries,” or similar.
- Open the dropdown using keyboard commands, then navigate the list of available bilingual dictionary options.
- Choose the desired language pair by pressing Enter or Space.
- Proceed to the search input field to type your query and search within the bilingual dictionary context.

This allows flexible language learning and searching in multiple language pairs on the Cambridge dictionary site, not just an English monolingual dictionary.[1][2][9]
