Here’s how you can search for a specific word on the Cambridge English Dictionary homepage:

---

## 👀 Visual Instructions
1. Look at the **top of the page** — in the header area.  
2. You’ll see a **search box** with placeholder text like *“Search English”*.  
3. Click inside the search box.  
4. Type the word you want to look up (for example, *“culture”*).  
5. Press the **Enter key** on your keyboard, or click the **magnifying glass/search icon** next to the box.  
6. The page will load the dictionary entry for that word.

---

## 🎧 Screen Reader Instructions
1. Use **Tab** or the shortcut **“/”** until your screen reader announces: *“Search English, edit text.”*  
2. Type the word you want (e.g., *“culture”*).  
3. Press **Enter**.  
   - The screen reader will then load the results page for that word.  
4. Once the new page opens, use **heading navigation** to move through the definition, pronunciation, and examples.  
   - The word itself will be announced as a heading.  
   - Pronunciation buttons will be announced as *“Play button.”*  
   - Definitions and examples will be read as text under headings.

---

✅ So: **type your word into the search box at the top and press Enter**.
