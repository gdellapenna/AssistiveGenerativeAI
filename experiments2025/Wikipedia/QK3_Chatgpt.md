### Identity and Reign

* Merenre Nemtyemsaf I (meaning “Beloved of Ra, Nemty is his protection”) was an ancient Egyptian pharaoh of the Sixth Dynasty (Old Kingdom). ([Wikipedia][1])
* He is placed as the **fourth king** of the Sixth Dynasty. ([Wikipedia][1])
* He succeeded his father Pepi I Meryre and was succeeded by Pepi II Neferkare. ([Wikipedia][1])
* His reign is dated to the early 23rd century BC (various estimates) and is typically credited at around **6 to 11 years**. ([Wikipedia][1])

---

### Family and Succession

* Father: Pepi I Meryre. Mother: Queen Ankhesenpepi I. ([Wikipedia][1])
* He married his aunt/step-mother, Queen Ankhesenpepi II, who was also mother of Pepi II. Thus, Pepi II may have been his son (though this is not certain). ([Wikipedia][1])
* He had at least one daughter, Ankhesenpepi III, and possibly another child Iput II. ([Wikipedia][1])

---

### Monuments and Archaeological Attestation

* His pyramid complex is located at Saqqara and is known by its ancient name **Khanefermerenre** (“The appearance/perfection of Merenre”). ([Wikipedia][1])
* The pyramid originally had a base of about 79 m and height of about 52.5 m; the layout forms a 3-4-5 Pythagorean triangle (75/100/125 in Egyptian cubits) indicating the slope. ([Wikipedia][1])
* Archaeological artefacts bearing his name include ivory boxes, alabaster vessels, statues and other items in major museums (Louvre, British Museum, etc.). ([Wikipedia][1])

---

### Domestic & Foreign Activities

* **Domestic:** He initiated construction works (his pyramid, possibly a Ka-chapel at Abydos) and administrative reforms: an increase of provincial administrators, suggesting shifts in governance away from centralised power. ([Wikipedia][1])
* **Foreign / Resource campaigns:**

  * Mining expeditions in the Eastern Desert (Wadi Hammamat, Hatnub) under his oversight. ([Wikipedia][1])
  * Activity in Lower Nubia: rock reliefs show his subjugation of Nubian chiefs; a canal with seven boats built to improve navigation near the First Cataract. ([Wikipedia][1])

---

### Chronology & Co-regency Issues

* There is some debate whether he had a co-regency with his father Pepi I, but many Egyptologists consider the evidence inconclusive and favour a direct succession. ([Wikipedia][1])
* The exact length of his reign is uncertain: inscriptions hint at a decade (10–11 years) but other sources favour shorter (6–7) years. ([Wikipedia][1])

---

### Burial & Mummy

* His pyramid has a mortuary complex; the burial chamber once held a black basalt sarcophagus and possibly a mummy (found in the 19th century) but the identity is uncertain due to wrapping style and condition. ([Wikipedia][1])
* The mortuary cult for him persisted at least until the end of the Old Kingdom; his name appears in later king lists from the New Kingdom. ([Wikipedia][1])

---

### Legacy

* Merenre was honoured with a state-sponsored funerary cult in his pyramid complex. ([Wikipedia][1])
* In later periods his name appears on king lists (such as the Karnak King List) indicating enduring recognition. ([Wikipedia][1])
