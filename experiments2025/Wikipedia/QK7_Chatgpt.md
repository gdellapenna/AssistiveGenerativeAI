Yes — you can search for any Wikipedia entry directly from the Main Page. Here’s how to do it step by step:

---

### Visual instructions (for sighted users)

1. Look near the **top-right area of the page** (or in the left sidebar in some layouts) for the **search box**.

   * It’s a small rectangular field labeled “Search Wikipedia” or “Search”.
2. Click inside the **search box** to place your cursor there.
3. Type the name of the entry you want to search for.

   * For example: “Albert Einstein”.
4. Press **Enter** or click the **magnifying glass icon** next to the box.
5. The page will reload with search results. If there is an exact match, it may take you directly to the article.

---

### Screen‑reader instructions

1. Press **Tab** repeatedly until you hear something like: “Search edit” or “Search Wikipedia edit”.

   * This is the search text box.
2. Type the entry you want to search for.

   * Example: “Albert Einstein”.
3. Press **Enter**.
4. After pressing Enter, the page will load either:

   * The article directly (if there is an exact match), or
   * A search results page with multiple links.
5. On the search results page:

   * Use **H** (heading navigation) to locate the “Search results” heading, or
   * Use **K** (NVDA/JAWS link navigation) to hear each link one by one.
6. When you hear the link for the article you want, press **Enter** to open it.
