## 🧭 Summary of Merenre Nemtyemsaf I

- **Identity**: Merenre Nemtyemsaf I was an ancient Egyptian pharaoh of the Sixth Dynasty.
- **Reign**: He ruled for one to two years in the late 23rd century BCE.
- **Succession**: He succeeded his father, Pepi I, and was followed by his brother, Pepi II.
- **Historical Record**:
  - His name appears in the **Abydos King List** and the **Royal Canon of Turin**.
  - He is mentioned in inscriptions from the tomb of **Weni the Elder**, a governor of Upper Egypt.
- **Legacy**: His short reign is considered a transitional period between two long-reigning pharaohs.
