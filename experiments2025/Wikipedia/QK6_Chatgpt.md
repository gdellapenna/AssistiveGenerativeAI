Yes — Wikipedia provides a way to export pages to PDF, but it is not immediately visible as a “PDF” button on the main page. On the Main Page specifically, you need to use the **“Print/export” menu** in the sidebar. Here’s how:

---

### Visual instructions

1. Look at the **left-hand sidebar** (navigation menu). Scroll down toward the bottom until you find a section titled **“Print/export”**.
2. Under “Print/export”, you will see two options:

   * **Download as PDF**
   * **Printable version**
3. Click **“Download as PDF”**.
4. The page will open a new interface where you can generate a PDF. Usually there’s a **“Download”** or **“Generate PDF”** button. Click that to create the PDF.
5. Your browser will then download the PDF file of the page.

---

### Screen‑reader instructions

1. Press **H** repeatedly until you hear a heading labelled **“Print/export”**.
2. Under that heading, you will find two links:

   * “Download as PDF link”
   * “Printable version link”
3. Use **Tab** or the link navigation command (**K** in NVDA/JAWS) to select **“Download as PDF link”**. Press **Enter**.
4. A new page/interface will open announcing something like “PDF download setup” or “PDF generation page”.
5. Navigate by headings or tabs to find the **“Download”** or **“Generate PDF”** button. Press **Enter** to create and download the PDF.
6. Wait until the screen reader announces the browser has started the download; the file should appear in your default downloads folder.
