Here is a summary of the main sections on the Wikipedia “Main Page” and what each contains. I’ll first name the section (in order as they appear) and then describe its contents.

---

1. **“From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia” / Welcome message**

   * This is the topmost portion of the page. It introduces Wikipedia as “the free encyclopedia that anyone can edit.” ([Wikipedia][1])
   * It also gives a quick statistics line (for example showing the number of active editors, number of articles in English) at the time of viewing. ([Wikipedia][1])
   * This is more of a header / context section rather than a content-list section.

2. **“From today’s featured article”**

   * This section shows a specific article chosen as the featured one for the day. It includes:

     * The title of the article (bold)
     * An image associated with the article. ([Wikipedia][1])
     * A short extract / summary of the article’s beginning paragraphs. ([Wikipedia][1])
     * A link labelled **“Full article…”** that takes you to the complete article. ([Wikipedia][1])
   * The idea: highlight one specific article each day to draw attention.
   * In the current version: the featured article is the Royal Artillery Memorial, a war memorial in London, including its history and design. ([Wikipedia][1])

3. **“Did you know…”**

   * This section contains a list of short “Did you know” facts — interesting tidbits or lesser-known details, each linking to an article for more information. ([Wikipedia][1])
   * Example entries: one about the McLaren MCL38 Formula One car, another about hypnosis in fiction, etc. ([Wikipedia][1])
   * At the end of the list there is also a link labelled **“Archive”** and a link for **“Start a new article”** and **“Nominate an article”**. ([Wikipedia][1])

4. **“In the news”**

   * This section gives current events: short bullet points about recent news items, each linking to the related Wikipedia article. ([Wikipedia][1])
   * There is also a sub-line labelled **“Ongoing:”** followed by links to ongoing major events or crises. ([Wikipedia][1])
   * And then **“Recent deaths”** links to persons who have recently died. ([Wikipedia][1])
   * A link **“More current events”** is present at the end. ([Wikipedia][1])

5. **“On this day”**

   * This section lists historical events that occurred on the same date in past years (anniversary-style). ([Wikipedia][1])
   * For example: in this version, events on November 9 such as battles, blizzards, political adoptions of flags, etc. ([Wikipedia][1])
   * At the end there are links like **“More anniversaries”** and **“Archive”**. ([Wikipedia][1])

6. **“Today’s featured picture”**

   * This section displays a high-quality or interesting image selected for the day. It includes:

     * The image itself (often large) and a caption describing what is shown. ([Wikipedia][1])
     * A short paragraph giving background information about the image. For instance: in this version, an image of the Kumaon region in India and related description. ([Wikipedia][1])
     * A list of links like **“More featured pictures”** and an **“Archive”**. ([Wikipedia][1])

7. **“Other areas of Wikipedia”**

   * This section provides links to different functional parts of Wikipedia (for both readers and editors). For example: *Community portal*, *Village pump*, *Site news*, *Help desk*, *Reference desk*, *Content portals*. ([Wikipedia][1])
   * These are basically navigation resources rather than main content.

8. **“Wikipedia’s sister projects”**

   * Here are links to other Wikimedia Foundation hosted projects (for example Wikimedia Commons, MediaWiki, Wikibooks, Wikidata, etc.). ([Wikipedia][1])
   * Each with a short description: e.g., “Free media repository” for Commons. ([Wikipedia][1])

9. **“Wikipedia languages”**

   * This section lists links to other language editions of Wikipedia (showing the largest ones first). It explains that the English Wikipedia is this one, and many others exist. ([Wikipedia][1])
   * It lists many languages like Arabic, German, Spanish, French, Italian, etc. ([Wikipedia][1])

10. **Footer / Legal & Miscellaneous**

    * At the very bottom are typical items: legal notices (license statement “Text is available under the Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0 License”), last edited date and time, links to about, disclaimers, privacy policy. ([Wikipedia][1])
    * Also there is text like “This page was last edited on 31 October 2025 at 23:59 (UTC).” ([Wikipedia][1])
