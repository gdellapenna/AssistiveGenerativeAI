### **Reported Information:**

* **Identity:** **Merenre Nemtyemsaf I** (often called **Merenre I** by Egyptologists).
* **Time Period:** He died around **2272 BC**.
* **Reign:** He was an **Ancient Egyptian pharaoh**, the **fourth king of the Sixth Dynasty**. He ruled Egypt for **six to eleven years** in the early **23rd century BC**, toward the end of the **Old Kingdom** period.
* **Family:**
    * **Father:** His predecessor, **Pepi I Meryre**.
    * **Mother:** Queen **Ankhesenpepi I**.
    * **Successor:** **Pepi II Neferkare** (who might have been his son or less probably his brother).
    * **Co-regency:** Pepi I may have shared power with Merenre at the very end of Pepi I's reign.
* **Administration/Policy:** His rule saw profound changes in the administration of the southern provinces of Egypt, including a **marked increase in the number of provincial administrators** and a concurrent **steep decline in the size of the central administration** in the capital, **Memphis**.
* **Monument:** A **pyramid complex** was built for him in **Saqqara** and was likely completed before his death.
