Yes — you *can* switch to another language on the Wikimedia Foundation “Wikipedia” main page. Here’s how, both visually and via screen reader:

---

### Visual instructions

1. Scroll toward the bottom of the page (or near the left- or right-side, depending on the layout) until you find a list of language names.

   * You will see a list of links showing the names of languages (for example: Deutsch, Español, Français, Italiano, Nederlands, Русский, etc.).
2. Click the link for the language you want.

   * For example, click “Italiano” to switch to the Italian version.
3. The browser will open the corresponding version of Wikipedia in that language, which is a separate site (for example it.wikipedia.org for Italiano).

---

### Screen-reader instructions

1. While on the page, press **H** repeatedly until you hear a heading labelled something like “Languages” (or perhaps “Languages” list).

   * On some screen readers you might navigate by landmarks/regions: press **Insert + F6** (if using NVDA) or **VO + F6** (VoiceOver) and look for a landmark labelled “Other languages” or “Languages”.
2. Once you are inside the language links list, use the link-navigation command (for example **K** in NVDA/JAWS, or **Ctrl + Option + L** in VoiceOver) to move through links. You will hear something like: “Deutsch link”, “Español link”, “Français link”, “Italiano link”, etc.
3. When you hear the name of the language you want (for example “Italiano link”), press **Enter** to activate it.
4. The page will reload and change to that language’s Wikipedia edition.

   * If your screen reader announces the new page’s heading (e.g., “Wikipedia, l’enciclopedia libera…” in Italian), you know the switch succeeded.
