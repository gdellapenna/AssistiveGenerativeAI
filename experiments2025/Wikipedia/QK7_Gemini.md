Searching for a specific article or entry is done using the **Search** feature, which is one of the most prominent elements on the page.

### **How to Search for a Specific Entry**

#### **Visual Directions (General User)**

1.  Look near the top-center of the page.
2.  There is a search bar labeled "**Search**" next to a magnifying glass icon.
3.  Click inside the search bar (the text input field).
4.  Type the name of the entry you are looking for (e.g., "Eiffel Tower").
5.  Press the **Enter** key, or click the magnifying glass icon to submit your search.

#### **Screen Reader Directions (Blind User)**

1.  **Use the "Next Form Element" or "Edit Field" command** (the exact command varies, but often **E** or **F** depending on your reader configuration) to quickly find the interactive fields.
2.  Navigate until your screen reader announces an **"Edit" field** or **"Search" input field**. It should be explicitly labeled or placed right next to a **"Search" button**.
3.  **Activate the edit field** (usually by pressing **Enter**).
4.  **Type your search term** (e.g., "Eiffel Tower").
5.  When you are done typing, you can either:
    * **Press the Enter key** on your keyboard to submit the search.
    * **Use the "Next Button" or "Next Link" command** to find the **"Search" button** (or the magnifying glass icon, which acts as the submit button) and then press **Enter** on that button.

The system will then take you to the article or a page of search results.
