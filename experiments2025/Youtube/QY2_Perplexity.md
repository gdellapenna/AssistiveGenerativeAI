On the YouTube search results page for "accessibility web" (https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=accessibility+web), here are step-by-step instructions for both sighted and screen reader users:

### Directions based on visible content:
- The page shows a list of video results related to your search, each including a video title link, channel name, view count, and upload date.
- Use the Tab key on your keyboard to move focus through clickable elements sequentially.
- The first interactive element near the top will be the YouTube logo (link to homepage), followed by the search box, then filter options (like upload date, type, duration).
- After the filters, individual video results appear in a vertical list.
- You can press Enter when on a video title to start playing that video.
- At the bottom or top, you may find pagination controls if there are multiple pages of results to navigate through next sets of videos.
- The left side typically contains the main navigation menu (Home, Explore, Subscriptions, Library).
- Use the keyboard arrow keys or Tab key to navigate through these menus and video items.

### Directions for screen reader users:
- On page load, the screen reader announces the main landmarks and headings: header with logo and search input, main search results area, and navigation sidebar.
- Use the screen reader’s "Next Heading" or "Jump to Heading" command to quickly move to the search results heading.
- Then use "Next Link" or "Next Button" command to move through each video title link sequentially.
- Press Enter/Return on the selected video title link to open and play the video.
- Use "Next Landmark" or "Next Region" commands to access filters and navigation menus.
- To adjust the search or perform another search, use the landmark or heading for search input, enter your query, and then submit.
- Pagination links, if any, are usually labeled like "Next" or "Page 2" and can be tabbed or jumped to for continued browsing.
- YouTube’s search results page is designed with heading structure and ARIA landmarks that support screen reader navigation and keyboard control.
