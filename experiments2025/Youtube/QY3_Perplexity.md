The YouTube search results page for the query "accessibility web" is structured to present video results related to that query. The main content of the page consists of a vertical list of video entries. Each entry includes the video title (a clickable link), the channel name that uploaded the video, view count, and the upload date. At the top, there is a search box where you can modify or enter new search queries. Just below the search box are options for filtering the results by criteria such as relevance, upload date, type of video, duration, and features.

On the left side of the page, there is a navigation menu containing links to YouTube’s main sections like Home, Explore, Subscriptions, and Library. The page also typically includes pagination or scrolling to load more video results beyond what first appears.

This page uses headings, ARIA landmarks, and link elements that are accessible by screen readers. These features enable users to navigate through search results by jumping between headings and links, making it possible to locate videos quickly, listen to their titles, and activate playback without needing a mouse.
