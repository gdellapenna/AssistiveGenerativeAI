To use a web page with a screen reader effectively, you can follow these basic steps presented in two formats: a simple direction focused on visible page content, and a way to engage with the page using a screen reader.

If you open a typical web page, start by familiarizing yourself with its main sections. Look for headings, which are usually large or bold text that organize topics on the page. You can move through headings to quickly understand what sections are available. Next, locate links that take you to other pages or sections of the same page. Identify buttons or form fields if you need to interact, such as filling out a form or submitting information.

Using a screen reader, navigate primarily by heading structure. Press the "H" key to move forward to the next heading and "Shift+H" to move backward to previous headings. When you reach a heading of interest, use arrow keys to read the text following that heading. To skip directly to links, press the "K" key to go forward or "Shift+K" to go backward through links. For form fields, use a key combination like "Insert+F5" (in JAWS) to list and jump to available form fields. You can use arrow keys to read text line-by-line and "Spacebar" to activate buttons or checkboxes. This structural approach enables efficient scanning and use of the page's content.

These methods support understanding the page layout and finding needed information quickly, even if you cannot view the page visually.
