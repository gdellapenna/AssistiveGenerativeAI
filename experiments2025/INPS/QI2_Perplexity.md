The INPS homepage features several menus that organize navigation and access to different parts of the site:

1. A top-level main menu that typically includes:
   - "Accedi a MyINPS" – access to your personal area with services and information.
   - Sections linking to key categories such as:
     - "Qual è la tua necessità?" — guiding users to services based on profiles and needs.
     - News and updates.
     - "Contatti INPS" — contact options including phone and digital channels.

2. Within the MyINPS personal area (accessed via the "Accedi a MyINPS" menu item):
   - A left-side navigation menu for:
     - "Bacheca" (dashboard),
     - "I tuoi dati" (your data),
     - "Centro notifiche" (notification center),
     - "I tuoi servizi e strumenti" (your chosen services and tools),
     - "I tuoi preferiti" (your favorites),
     - "Contatti INPS" (contact channels).

3. Service menus:
   - "I più utilizzati" on the homepage shows the most frequently used services as direct links.
   - Various menus organizing all services by categories for specific needs.

The site uses a hierarchical structure with headings marking each menu and sub-menu section, making it accessible to screen readers. The main navigation is located near the top or left areas. Contact information menus provide phone numbers and digital contact points for support.

For a screen reader, menus can be navigated by jumping through headings ("H" key) and links ("K" key) systematically:
- The main menu is identified by its heading and the links under it.
- The personal area menu (MyINPS) appears as another heading with its own navigation links.
- Other menus like contact details or frequently used services follow under their respective headings.

This menu structure ensures users can find services, personal account features, latest news, and support options quickly and efficiently with standard navigation commands.[1][2][6]
