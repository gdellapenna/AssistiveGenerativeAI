On the Google homepage (https://www.google.com/), the main task you can accomplish is performing internet searches by typing keywords, questions, or phrases into the central search bar. This search bar lets you find relevant information across the web quickly. 

Additionally, you can access various Google services from this page using links located near the top or corners of the page. Typical services include Google Images for image-based searches, Google Maps for location and directions, and Google News for current events, among others. The page may also feature a daily changing "Google Doodle," which is usually an image or animation commemorating special events or notable figures; clicking on it leads to more information about the doodle's subject.

There is a language selector typically found at the bottom right corner that allows you to change the display language of the page. Signing in with a Google account on this page enables personalized search experiences and access to other integrated Google services like Gmail and Drive.

For screen reader users: The search bar is the primary interactive element and is found centrally near the top of the page. Use the keyboard to tab or navigate headings, and press Enter after typing your search query to start the search. To access other services, navigate to the top navigation links by pressing the Tab key multiple times until you reach the list of service links (these might be labeled as "Images," "Maps," "News," etc.). The language switching option is near the bottom right of the page; you can tab through footer links to reach it. The Google Doodle will be near the search bar as an image with a link—your screen reader will announce it as a clickable graphic. Signing in is usually found at the top right corner, accessible via Tab navigation.

This homepage acts primarily as a gateway to Google's powerful search and services ecosystem.[1]
