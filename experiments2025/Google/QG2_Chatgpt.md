## ✅ What kind of tasks you can accomplish via Google’s homepage

On the Google homepage you can:

* Perform web searches: type in a query to find web pages matching your interest (e.g. information, news, articles).
* Search for images: using the “Images” (or “Immagini” / “Images”) link at the top of the page — useful when you are looking for pictures rather than web-pages.
* Change interface language to English (via a link typically labelled “English” or similar) so the page becomes easier to navigate in English.
* Sign in to your Google account (via “Sign in” / “Accedi”) to access personalized services — e.g. saved search history, account-specific preferences.
* Access other Google pages/links: through links like “Privacy”, “Terms”, “Settings” etc., which lead you to information about privacy, usage terms, cookies, search settings.
* Use advanced search features and filters when searching: after you submit a search, the results page will allow you to filter or refine results (e.g. safe-search filtering, domain-specific searches, etc.). ([Google Assistenza][1])
* Use special search operators if you know them (e.g. “site:”, “link:”, etc.) to narrow down results to specific domains or types of pages. ([google.it][2])

Additionally, via search results, you can get a variety of content types — not only web pages, but direct answers (knowledge, facts), maps and directions, images, sometimes interactive items (depending on query and result). ([Google][3])

## 🔎 Why these tasks are useful (especially given your context)

Because you are visually impaired and use a screen reader (per our settings), Google’s homepage serves as a **gateway** to almost all of the web, letting you quickly search for information by typing or speaking (depending on accessibility), rather than navigating websites manually.

* Using search reduces the need to know specific addresses of websites.
* You can switch the interface to English (or another language) to make navigation easier for you.
* After search, you can use results to access articles, definitions, news, references, educational content, etc.
* If you sign in, you can benefit from personalization — though if you prefer privacy, you can avoid logging in and still search as a guest.
* For children (you mentioned you have a child) or adult-safe content, you could use filters (e.g. safe search) to avoid explicit content. ([Safety Center][4])

## ⚠️ What you might *not* be able to do (or may need extra care)

* On the blank homepage itself, there is not much content beyond the search box and a few links — you cannot do deeper tasks until you issue a search.
* Some features (like background image personalization or “doodle” interactive logos) may depend on browser support or your screen reader — they might not be accessible or announced. Indeed, the “classic” homepage is simple for accessibility. ([blog.google][5])
* If your screen reader doesn’t detect dynamically loaded elements (because of JavaScript), the search box might be hard to locate until focus shifts — you may need to use keyboard navigation to find it.
* Advanced search filters, safe-search settings or personalized account settings (history, preferences) often require you to sign in — which may add complexity.
