On the Google homepage (https://www.google.com/), the main buttons and their functions are as follows:

- The central "Google Search" button: After you type a query in the search box, pressing this button submits your search to find relevant web results.
- The adjacent "I'm Feeling Lucky" button: This takes you directly to the top-ranked result for your query without showing a search results page.
- The microphone button within or next to the search box: Activates voice search to let you speak your query instead of typing.
- The "Images" link/button at the top right: Takes you to Google Images, where you can perform image-based searches.
- The "Sign in" button at the top right: Allows signing into your Google account for personalized services.
- The "Apps" button (grid icon) at the top right corner: Opens a menu with links to various Google services like Gmail, Drive, Maps, News, and more.
- The "Privacy" and other legal links found at the footer bottom: These open pages with Google's privacy policy and terms of service.
- Occasionally a "Google Doodle" (clickable graphic near the search box): Links to a page explaining that day's special doodle topic or event.

For screen reader users:  
When loading the page, your screen reader will recognize and announce these buttons as interactive controls or links. Use Tab to cycle through the buttons in logical order—search input and search buttons first, then voice search, followed by top navigation service links like Images and Sign in. The Apps menu is announced as a button with a grid icon. The footer contains links labeled as “Privacy,” “Terms,” and others for legal information. The Doodle is a clickable image with alternate text describing its theme. Activating buttons or links is done by pressing Enter when focused.

These button functions provide quick access to Google’s primary search and service features, making navigation and search efficient and accessible.[1][4][11][12][13]
