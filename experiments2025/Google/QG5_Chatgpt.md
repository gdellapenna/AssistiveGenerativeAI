Here’s exactly how to search for something on the Google homepage ([https://www.google.com/](https://www.google.com/)), step by step — both visually and in a screen-reader friendly way.

---

## 🔎 Searching visually (what you would see)

1. Open the Google homepage.
2. In the center of the page, just below the Google logo, you should see a **large rectangular text box** — this is the **search input field**.
3. Click inside this box and type the word or phrase you want to search for.
4. After typing your query, you have two options:

   * Press the **Enter** key on your keyboard.
   * Or click the **“Google Search”** button below or next to the text box.
5. The page will then load a **search results page** with links, summaries, and sometimes images related to your query.
6. You can click on any link to visit that page or use additional filters like “News”, “Images”, or “Videos” at the top of the results page.

---

## 🔉 Searching using a screen reader (keyboard-friendly)

1. Open the Google homepage in your browser. Make sure your screen reader is active.
2. Use the screen reader’s shortcut to **list form fields** or **jump to input fields** — often pressing **F** or navigating by landmarks can bring you to the search box.
3. When the focus lands on the **search input field**, type your search query.
4. Press **Enter** to submit the query. This will load the search results page.
5. On the results page:

   * Use your screen reader’s command to **list links** (often “L”) to hear all the clickable results.
   * Navigate to the link you want using the arrow keys, then press **Enter** to open it.
   * You can also navigate by headings (shortcut “H”) or landmarks to find sections like “Images”, “News”, or “Videos”.
