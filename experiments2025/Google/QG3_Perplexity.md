Yes, you can access Google News from the Google homepage at https://www.google.com/ by clicking a "News" link or tab, typically located in the top navigation bar or directly below the central search bar among other service shortcuts like Images or Maps. This link redirects you to news.google.com for aggregated news stories.[1][11]

**Visual Directions**  
Look for the black navigation bar at the very top of the page (it may say "Google apps" or show icons). Click the "More" or "Apps" icon (grid of dots, usually top-right), then select "News" from the dropdown menu of services. Alternatively, find the "News" tab immediately below the search bar and click it to go directly to recent headlines.[1]

**Screen Reader Directions**  
Load the page and use your screen reader's heading navigation (H key in NVDA/JAWS) to jump to the main top landmark or banner region. Tab forward (Tab key) through the top links until you hear "Apps" or "More" (a button or link); press Enter to open the menu, then arrow down to "News" and press Enter. If a "News" tab is announced below the edit field (search bar), it's often the second or third tab after "All"—use Tab or Ctrl+Right Arrow (in some readers) to reach it and press Enter. Your screen reader will announce interactive elements like these clearly.[12][13][1]
