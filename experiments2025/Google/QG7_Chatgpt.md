Yes — you **can search using your voice** from the Google homepage, but it depends on your browser and microphone access. Here’s how it works.

---

## 🔎 Searching visually with voice

1. Open [https://www.google.com/](https://www.google.com/).
2. In the center of the page, near the search box, look for a **microphone icon** inside or next to the search field. This is the “voice search” button.
3. Click the **microphone icon**. Your browser may ask for permission to access your microphone — grant permission.
4. Speak your search query clearly. Google will process your voice input and display the search results page automatically.

---

## 🎧 Searching with voice using a screen reader

1. On the Google homepage, use your screen reader to **navigate to the search box**. Often, this can be done by listing form fields or jumping to landmarks.
2. Once focus is on the search box, look for a **button labelled “Search by voice”** or “microphone” (your screen reader should announce it).
3. Activate the microphone button by pressing **Enter**.
4. If the browser prompts for microphone permission, approve it.
5. Speak your search query clearly. Google will automatically process the voice input and load the search results page.
6. On the results page, use your screen reader to navigate links, headings, or sections to explore the results.

---

⚠️ **Notes and considerations:**

* If your screen reader blocks or interferes with voice input, you may need to temporarily disable it or use the keyboard to activate the microphone.
* Voice search requires a working microphone and a browser that supports it (Chrome, Edge, or Firefox usually work best).
* The main Google homepage is the usual starting point — voice search is not available in some minimal versions or cached snapshots.
