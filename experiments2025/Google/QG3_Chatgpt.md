Good question. From the page you gave (Google homepage — [https://www.google.com/](https://www.google.com/)), you *cannot directly see news content*. But yes — you *can* reach Google News from there. Here's **how you can** (and what to expect), both visually and for a screen-reader.

## ✅ Can you reach Google News from Google.com — yes, but indirectly

* The Google homepage itself doesn’t embed news stories.

* To get to Google News you must **go to the Google News website** (news.google.com) or use a “News” or “Notizie / News” tab/special link when you search. ([Semrush][1])

* Once on Google News, it shows an aggregated feed of headlines and articles from many publishers. ([Wikipedia][2])

In short: Google.com is the search engine’s starting page; Google News is a separate service that aggregates news.

---

## 🧭 How to get to Google News from Google.com — visual way

1. Open your browser to [https://www.google.com/](https://www.google.com/).
2. In the address bar — replace the “google.com” (or after it) with **“news.google.com”** — then press Enter.
3. That will load the Google News homepage, which lists top stories, categories (e.g. World, Italy, Tech, Sport), and a feed of recent articles.

Alternatively:

* First do any search on Google.com — then on the search-results page look if there’s a **“News / Notizie / News tab / News filter”** (on top or near filters). Clicking that will show news results rather than “All web” results; from there you can also click a link to “Visit Google News” or open stories.

---

## 🔉 How to do the same using a screen reader (keyboard navigation)

1. Open Google.com in your browser; make sure your screen reader is active.
2. Press `Ctrl + L` (or the command to move focus to address bar) — type `news.google.com` — press Enter.
3. The page will load — use your screen reader’s shortcut to list headings (commonly “H” key) or landmarks; this helps locate the main news feed or navigation.
4. Use arrow keys or “links list” (often “L”) to navigate through article headlines — each will be a link you can activate (press Enter) to open.
5. If you want a different news category (e.g. “World news”, “Technology” etc.), use headings or navigation links near the top/side of the page to jump to the category list, then activate the desired category.

---

## ⚠️ Why you don’t see news on Google.com itself

* The main Google homepage is primarily for search — it doesn’t embed news content by default.
* The news-aggregation service is separate and lives at a different URL (news.google.com).
