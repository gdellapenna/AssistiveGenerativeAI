Yes, you can search using your voice on the Google homepage at https://www.google.com/ by clicking the microphone icon next to the central search bar, which activates voice input if your device has microphone access granted. Speak your query clearly after the icon activates (often with a visual or audio prompt), and Google will process it to display results.[1][2]

**Visual Directions**  
Find the large central search bar. To its right, inside or adjacent to the bar, locate the small microphone icon (it may appear gray until hovered). Click the microphone to start listening, speak your search terms (e.g., "weather today"), and wait for results to appear automatically once you stop speaking. Grant microphone permission if prompted by your browser.[1]

**Screen Reader Directions**  
After page load, tab to the main search edit field (often auto-focused, announced as "Search, edit" with a microphone button nearby). Use arrow keys or Tab to reach the adjacent "microphone" or "Search by voice" button/link; your screen reader will announce it clearly. Press Enter to activate—speak your query when the listening tone or announcement occurs (e.g., "listening"). Results load as with text search; ensure browser microphone permissions via Tab to site settings if needed.[11][12][1]
