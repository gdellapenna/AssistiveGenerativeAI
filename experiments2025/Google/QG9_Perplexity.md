On the Google homepage at https://www.google.com/, buttons directly under the central search bar typically include tabs or shortcuts for quick access to specialized search modes, such as "All," "Images," "News," "Shopping," and "Maps." These allow switching search contexts without leaving the page—e.g., "All" for general web results, "Images" for visual searches, "News" for headlines, "Shopping" for product finds, and "Maps" for locations.[3][11]

**Visual Directions**  
Immediately below the search input field, look for a row of labeled tabs or buttons (often blue-outlined when active). Click any to filter your upcoming search (e.g., click "Images" before typing to search pictures only). They change dynamically based on personalization or trends.[11]

**Screen Reader Directions**  
After the search edit field (auto-focused on load), Tab forward or use Ctrl+Right Arrow (in NVDA/JAWS) to reach the tab list announced as buttons or links like "All," "Images," etc. Arrow through them for details, then press Enter on one to activate the mode—your screen reader will confirm the switch. They form a grouped interactive region for easy linear navigation.[12][13][3]
