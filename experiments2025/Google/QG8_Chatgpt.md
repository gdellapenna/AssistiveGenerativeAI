On the current **Google homepage** ([https://www.google.com/](https://www.google.com/)), there are a few visible **buttons**, each serving a specific purpose. I’ll break them down visually and then in screen‑reader terms.

## 🔎 Buttons on the page — visual overview

1. **“Google Search” button** (below or next to the search box)

   * Purpose: Submits your typed query to perform a web search.

2. **“I’m Feeling Lucky” button** (sometimes next to the search box)

   * Purpose: Directly opens the top search result for your query, bypassing the results page.

3. **Microphone icon (voice search)**

   * Purpose: Lets you speak your search query instead of typing it. Activates the browser microphone.

4. **Camera icon on Google Images page (not main homepage)**

   * Purpose: Lets you search using an image (upload or paste URL).

5. **Other footer buttons/links** — sometimes displayed as clickable buttons depending on the design:

   * **“Privacy”** → opens Google’s privacy policy.
   * **“Terms” / “Terms of Service”** → opens Google’s terms of use.
   * **“Settings” / “Impostazioni”** → opens search settings or language settings.
   * **“Feedback”** → opens a form to report feedback to Google.

> Note: On the main homepage, most of these appear as **links** rather than traditional buttons. Only the search box controls and voice search are “buttons” in the interactive sense.

---

## 🎧 Buttons for screen-reader users

* **Search buttons (“Google Search”, “I’m Feeling Lucky”)**:

  1. Navigate to the form controls or search input field using your screen reader shortcuts.
  2. The buttons will be announced by their labels (“Google Search” / “I’m Feeling Lucky”).
  3. Press **Enter** to activate the desired action.

* **Microphone / voice search button**:

  1. Use link/form navigation to find the button near the search field. Your screen reader may announce it as **“Search by voice”** or **“microphone button”**.
  2. Press **Enter** to start voice input.
  3. Speak your query clearly; Google will process it.

* **Footer “buttons” (links)**:

  1. Use the screen reader’s **link list** shortcut (often “L”) to list all links.
  2. Navigate to the one you want (Privacy, Terms, Settings, Feedback) and press **Enter** to open.
