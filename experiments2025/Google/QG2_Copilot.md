## 👀 Visual Navigation (Sighted User)
On *https://www.google.com/*, the main task is **searching the web**:
- In the **center of the page**, there’s a large **search box**. You can type any word, phrase, or question here.  
- Just below the search box are two buttons:  
  - **“Google Search”** → shows you a list of results related to your query.  
  - **“I’m Feeling Lucky”** → takes you directly to the first result without showing the list.  
- At the **top-right corner**, you can also:  
  - Click **Gmail** to go to Google’s email service.  
  - Click **Images** to search specifically for pictures.  
  - Click **Sign in** to log into your Google account.  
- At the **bottom of the page**, there are links for things like **Privacy**, **Terms**, and **About Google**.

So the primary task here is **searching for information on the web**, but you can also access Gmail, image search, or sign in.

---

## 🎧 Screen Reader Navigation (Blind User)
Here’s how you can accomplish the same tasks step by step:
- When the page loads, your screen reader announces **“Google”** as the page title.  
- **To perform a search:**  
  1. Press **Tab once** (or use your screen reader’s “Form field navigation” command).  
  2. Focus will move to the **search input field**. Type your query.  
  3. Press **Enter** to submit the search (this activates the “Google Search” button automatically).  
- **To use “I’m Feeling Lucky”:**  
  - After typing in the search box, press **Tab** until you reach the **“I’m Feeling Lucky” button**, then press **Enter**.  
- **To access Gmail, Images, or Sign in:**  
  - Use **Tab** repeatedly from the top of the page, or open your screen reader’s **Links List** and select the desired link.  
- **To reach footer links (Privacy, Terms, etc.):**  
  - Press **Tab** until you cycle through all interactive elements, or use the **Links List** shortcut to jump directly.
