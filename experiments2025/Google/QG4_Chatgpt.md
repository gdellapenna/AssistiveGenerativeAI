Good question. Yes — you *can* read the privacy information about Google from its homepage. Here’s **how** you do that, both visually and with a screen reader.

---

## 🔍 Where to find Google’s privacy info

* On the homepage you visited (google.com), there is a link in the footer labelled **“Privacy”** (or the equivalent in your interface language — e.g. “Privacy & Terms”, or in Italian “Privacy” / “Termini e condizioni”, depending on locale). This link leads to Google’s privacy‑policy page. ([blog.google][1])
* When you activate that link, you go to the page Google Privacy & Terms, which contains the full policy document. ([policies.google.com][2])

---

## ✔️ How to read the privacy info — visual instructions

1. Scroll down to the bottom (footer) of the homepage.
2. Look for a link labelled **“Privacy”** or **“Privacy & Terms”** (in Italian or whatever language the page is displayed in).
3. Click that link. The page will navigate to Google’s policy page.
4. On that page you can read sections such as “Privacy Policy”, “Terms of Service”, “Our privacy & security principles”, etc.

---

## 🎧 How to read the privacy info — screen‑reader instructions

1. On google.com, use the screen‑reader command to list *links* (often the “L” key) until you hear a link labelled “Privacy” or “Privacy & Terms”.
2. Press Enter or the screen‑reader’s “activate link” command to follow it.
3. Once the new page loads (the Privacy & Terms page), again use link‑list or heading‑list commands (e.g. “H” for headings, or “L” for links) to navigate the document. You’ll find entries like “Privacy Policy”, “Overview”, “Terms of Service”, etc. ([policies.google.com][2])
4. Use arrow‑down or page‑down to read through the sections, or use headings / section navigation if supported to jump between major parts (e.g. “Introduction”, “What information we collect”, “Your privacy controls”, etc.) ([policies.google.com][3])
