Yes, you can read privacy information about the Google homepage at https://www.google.com/ by accessing the "Privacy" link in the footer section at the bottom of the page, which leads to Google's full Privacy Policy explaining data collection, usage, and controls.[2]

**Visual Directions**  
Scroll to the very bottom of the page to the dark footer area. Look for the "Privacy" link among horizontal text links (others include "Terms," "Advertising," and "About Google"); it's typically the first or second link in the "More options" or legal section. Click it to open the policy in a new tab or page.[2]

**Screen Reader Directions**  
After loading the page, use your screen reader's footer or landmark navigation (Shift+H in NVDA/JAWS for bottom-up headings, or D for landmarks) to jump to the page footer region. Tab through the bottom links (or use K for next link) until you hear "Privacy" announced as a link; press Enter to activate it. The policy page will load with structured headings for easy navigation via H key.[11][12][2]
