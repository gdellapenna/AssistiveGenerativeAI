Yes — you **can search using images** from the Google homepage, but it requires navigating to **Google Images**, which is a separate interface linked from the homepage. Here’s how you can do it.

---

## 🔎 Searching visually using images

1. Open [https://www.google.com/](https://www.google.com/).
2. At the top-right (or top-center) of the page, look for a link labelled **“Images”** (in Italian, “Immagini”).
3. Click the **Images** link. This opens the Google Images page.
4. On the Google Images page, you will see a **camera icon** in the search box. This is the “search by image” feature.
5. Click the **camera icon**, then either:

   * **Paste the URL** of an image from the web, or
   * **Upload an image file** from your computer.
6. After uploading or pasting the image URL, Google will show search results related to that image — visually similar images and pages containing the image.

---

## 🎧 Searching using images with a screen reader

1. On Google.com, use your screen reader to **list links** (shortcut often “L”) until you find **“Images”** or “Immagini”.
2. Activate that link by pressing **Enter**.
3. On the Google Images page:

   * Use the screen reader to navigate to the **search form**. Look for a **button or link labelled “Search by image”** or **camera icon**.
   * Press Enter to activate the “search by image” dialog.
4. Depending on your browser and screen reader, you can:

   * **Paste the URL** of an image into the input field, then submit.
   * Or **upload a local image** (your screen reader should announce a “Choose file” or “Browse” button). Press Enter to open the file selection dialog, then navigate to the image file on your computer, select it, and confirm.
5. The results page will load with links to visually similar images and web pages containing that image. You can then use your screen reader to navigate links as usual.

---

⚠️ **Note:** The main Google.com homepage does **not** allow direct image uploads; you must first go to **Google Images** via the “Images/Immagini” link.
