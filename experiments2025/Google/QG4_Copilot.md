Excellent request 👍. The **Google homepage (https://www.google.com/)** does include access to privacy information, but it’s not immediately visible in the center. It’s located in the **footer area** of the page.

---

## 👀 Visual Navigation (Sighted User)
1. Scroll down to the **bottom of the page**.  
2. In the footer, you’ll see several links such as **Privacy**, **Terms**, and **Settings**.  
3. Click on the **“Privacy”** link.  
4. This will open Google’s Privacy Policy page, where you can read detailed information about how Google handles data.

---

## 🎧 Screen Reader Navigation (Blind User)
1. When the page loads, press **Tab repeatedly** until you reach the **footer links**.  
   - Alternatively, use your screen reader’s **Links List** command (often **Insert+F7** in NVDA or JAWS) to bring up all links on the page.  
2. In the list, locate the link labeled **“Privacy”**.  
3. Select it and press **Enter**.  
4. Your screen reader will then load the **Google Privacy Policy page**, where you can navigate through headings and text to read the information.

---
