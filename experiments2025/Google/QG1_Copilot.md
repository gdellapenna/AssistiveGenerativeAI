**Quick Answer:** The page at *https://www.google.com/* is the Google homepage. It mainly contains a large search box in the center, a few links at the top (like Gmail, Images, and Sign in), and footer links at the bottom (Privacy, Terms, About Google, etc.).  

---

## Step-by-Step Guidance

### 👀 Visual Navigation (for sighted users)
- **Center of the page:**  
  - You’ll see the Google logo at the top.  
  - Just below it is a large **search box** where you can type queries.  
  - Beneath the search box are two buttons: **“Google Search”** and **“I’m Feeling Lucky.”**
- **Top-right corner:**  
  - Links for **Gmail**, **Images**, and a **Sign in** button.  
- **Bottom of the page (footer):**  
  - Links like **Privacy**, **Terms**, **Advertising**, **Business Solutions**, and **About Google.**

### 🎧 Screen Reader Navigation
- When the page loads, your screen reader will announce **“Google”** as the page title.  
- **To reach the search box:**  
  - Press the **Tab key once** (or use your screen reader’s “Form field navigation” command).  
  - Focus will move to the **search input field**. You can type your query here.  
- **To reach the top links (Gmail, Images, Sign in):**  
  - Use the **Tab key repeatedly** from the top of the page, or use your screen reader’s “Links list” command to jump directly.  
- **To reach the footer links (Privacy, Terms, etc.):**  
  - Press **Tab** until you cycle through all interactive elements, or use the “Links list” shortcut to select them directly.  
- **Buttons under the search box:**  
  - After typing in the search field, press **Tab** to move to **“Google Search”** or **“I’m Feeling Lucky.”**  
  - Press **Enter** to activate the selected button.
