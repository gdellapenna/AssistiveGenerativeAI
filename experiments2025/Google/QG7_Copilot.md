Great question 👍. On the **Google homepage (https://www.google.com/)**, yes — you can search using your voice. This feature is called **Voice Search**, and it’s available directly in the search box.

---

## 👀 Visual Navigation (Sighted User)
1. In the **center of the page**, locate the large **search box** under the Google logo.  
2. On the **right side inside the search box**, you’ll see a small **microphone icon**.  
3. Click the **microphone icon**.  
4. A pop-up will appear asking for permission to use your computer’s microphone.  
5. Allow access.  
6. Speak your search query out loud (e.g., “weather in Rome”).  
7. Google will process your voice and display the search results.

---

## 🎧 Screen Reader Navigation (Blind User)
1. When the page loads, press **Tab once** to move focus to the **search input field**.  
2. Press **Tab again** to move to the **microphone button** (your screen reader will announce it as “Search by voice” or “Microphone”).  
3. Press **Enter** to activate it.  
4. Your browser will prompt you to allow microphone access.  
   - Use **Tab** to move to the **Allow button** and press **Enter**.  
5. Speak your query clearly.  
6. Google will automatically run the search and load the results page.  
7. Use your screen reader’s navigation commands (Tab, Links List, or Headings) to explore the results.

---
