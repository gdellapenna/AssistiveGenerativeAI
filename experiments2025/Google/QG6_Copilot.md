Good question 👍. On the **Google homepage (https://www.google.com/)**, you cannot directly upload or search with an image. The homepage only has a text search box.  

However, there is a way to reach **Google Images**, where you can perform searches using pictures.

---

## 👀 Visual Navigation (Sighted User)
1. Look at the **top-right corner** of the Google homepage.  
2. You’ll see a link labeled **“Images.”**  
3. Click on **“Images.”**  
4. This opens the **Google Images search page**.  
5. On that page, you’ll see a search box with a small **camera icon** inside it.  
6. Click the **camera icon** to either:  
   - Paste an image URL, or  
   - Upload an image from your computer.  
7. Google will then search using that image.

---

## 🎧 Screen Reader Navigation (Blind User)
1. When the homepage loads, press **Tab repeatedly** until you reach the link labeled **“Images.”**  
   - Alternatively, open your screen reader’s **Links List** (e.g., Insert+F7 in NVDA/JAWS) and select **“Images.”**  
2. Press **Enter** to open the **Google Images search page**.  
3. On the new page, move focus to the **search input field**.  
4. Your screen reader will announce a **camera button** next to the search box.  
5. Activate the **camera button** by tabbing to it and pressing **Enter**.  
6. You’ll then have options to:  
   - Paste an image URL, or  
   - Upload an image file from your device.  
7. Once you provide the image, Google will return search results based on it.

---

✅ **Summary:** You cannot search with images directly on the Google homepage, but you can access **Google Images** via the **“Images” link** in the top-right corner, and from there use the camera icon to search by image.
