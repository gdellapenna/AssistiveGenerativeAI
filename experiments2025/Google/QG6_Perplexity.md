Yes, you can search using images, but not directly on the main Google homepage. To search by image, you need to go to Google Images by clicking the "Images" link, usually located at the top right corner of the Google homepage. On the Google Images page, you will find a small camera icon inside the search bar which you can use to upload an image or paste an image URL to perform an image-based search. 

Alternatively, some browsers allow right-clicking on any image on a webpage and selecting "Search with Google Lens" or similar options to perform an image search directly.

For screen reader users: After arriving on the Google homepage, tab through the top navigation links until you hear "Images." Press Enter to go to the Google Images page. There, the search bar includes a "Search by image" button (announced as a button with a camera icon). Press Enter to open options to upload an image or paste a URL to search by image. If using the browser's context menu, right-click on any image, use arrow keys to find "Search with Google Lens," and press Enter to start an image search.

The main Google homepage itself focuses on text-based searching; image search is a secondary page accessed via the "Images" link.[2][3][9]
