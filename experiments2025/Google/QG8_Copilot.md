On the **Google homepage (https://www.google.com/)**, there are only a few buttons, and each has a clear purpose. Let’s go through them carefully.

---

## 👀 Visual Navigation (Sighted User)
- **Under the search box (center of the page):**  
  - **“Google Search” button** → Runs a search for whatever you typed in the search box and shows you a list of results.  
  - **“I’m Feeling Lucky” button** → Skips the results page and takes you directly to the first result Google finds.  
- **Inside the search box (right side):**  
  - **Microphone icon (Voice Search)** → Lets you speak your query instead of typing it.  
- **Top-right corner:**  
  - **“Sign in” button** → Opens a login page where you can enter your Google account credentials.  

---

## 🎧 Screen Reader Navigation (Blind User)
- **To reach the search buttons:**  
  1. Press **Tab once** to move into the search box. Type your query.  
  2. Press **Tab again** to move to the **“Google Search” button**. Press **Enter** to run the search.  
  3. Press **Tab once more** to reach the **“I’m Feeling Lucky” button**. Press **Enter** to go directly to the first result.  
- **To use Voice Search:**  
  - After focusing on the search box, press **Tab** until your screen reader announces **“Search by voice”** or **“Microphone.”** Press **Enter** to activate it, then allow microphone access and speak your query.  
- **To sign in:**  
  - Press **Tab repeatedly** until your screen reader announces **“Sign in.”** Press **Enter** to open the login page.

---

✅ **Summary:**  
- **Google Search** → shows results.  
- **I’m Feeling Lucky** → jumps straight to the first result.  
- **Microphone icon** → lets you search by voice.  
- **Sign in** → logs you into your Google account.
