Perfect, let’s walk through exactly how to **perform a search on the Google homepage (https://www.google.com/)**.

---

## 👀 Visual Navigation (Sighted User)
1. In the **center of the page**, locate the large **search box** just below the Google logo.  
2. Click inside the search box with your mouse.  
3. Type the word, phrase, or question you want to search for (e.g., “weather in Rome”).  
4. After typing, you have two options:  
   - Press **Enter** on your keyboard → this will immediately run the search.  
   - Or click the **“Google Search”** button just below the box → this does the same thing.  
5. The page will reload with a list of search results related to your query.

---

## 🎧 Screen Reader Navigation (Blind User)
1. When the page loads, your screen reader announces **“Google”** as the page title.  
2. Press **Tab once** (or use your screen reader’s “Form field navigation” command) to move focus to the **search input field**.  
   - Your screen reader will announce something like **“Search, edit text”**.  
3. Type your query (e.g., “weather in Rome”).  
4. Press **Enter**.  
   - This automatically activates the **Google Search** function.  
   - You don’t need to tab to the “Google Search” button; pressing Enter in the search field is enough.  
5. Your screen reader will then load the **search results page**, announcing headings and links.  
   - Use **Tab** or your screen reader’s **Links List** command to explore the results.  
   - Press **Enter** on any link to open that result.

---

✅ **Summary:** To search, type your query into the central search box and press **Enter**. Sighted users can click the “Google Search” button, but for screen reader users, pressing Enter in the search field is the fastest way.
