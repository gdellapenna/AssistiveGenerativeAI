On the **current Google homepage** ([https://www.google.com/](https://www.google.com/)), there are usually **two buttons directly under the search bar**. Here’s a detailed explanation:

---

## 🔎 Visual overview of the buttons under the search bar

1. **“Google Search”**

   * **Purpose:** Performs a standard Google search for whatever you typed in the search box.
   * After clicking this button (or pressing Enter while focused in the search box), you will be taken to the search results page, which lists relevant web pages, articles, and sometimes direct answers.

2. **“I’m Feeling Lucky”**

   * **Purpose:** Skips the search results page entirely and takes you **directly to the top search result** for your query.
   * Example: If you type “Wikipedia” and click this, you will go straight to the Wikipedia homepage instead of seeing a results page.

> Note: The **microphone icon** for voice search is often visually inside the search bar, but is separate from these two buttons.

---

## 🎧 Screen-reader instructions

1. Use your screen reader to **navigate to the form controls** or **jump to the search box**.
2. Once you have typed your search query:

   * Press **Tab** once (or use form navigation) to move to the **“Google Search” button**. The screen reader will announce something like **“Google Search, button”**. Press **Enter** to perform the standard search.
   * Press **Tab** again to move to the **“I’m Feeling Lucky” button**. The screen reader will announce **“I’m Feeling Lucky, button”**. Press **Enter** to go directly to the top result.

> Tip: If you use a keyboard only, pressing **Enter** while focused in the search box performs the same action as clicking **“Google Search”**.
