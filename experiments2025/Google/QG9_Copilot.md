On the **Google homepage (https://www.google.com/)**, there are two main buttons directly under the search bar. Here’s what they do:

---

## 👀 Visual Navigation (Sighted User)
- **“Google Search” button**  
  - Located just below the search box, slightly to the left.  
  - Purpose: Runs a search for whatever you typed in the search box and shows you a list of results.  

- **“I’m Feeling Lucky” button**  
  - Located just below the search box, slightly to the right of “Google Search.”  
  - Purpose: Skips the results page and takes you directly to the first result Google finds for your query.  

---

## 🎧 Screen Reader Navigation (Blind User)
- After typing your query in the search box:  
  1. Press **Tab** once → focus moves to the **“Google Search” button**.  
     - Screen reader announces **“Google Search, button.”**  
     - Press **Enter** to run the search and see results.  
  2. Press **Tab** again → focus moves to the **“I’m Feeling Lucky” button**.  
     - Screen reader announces **“I’m Feeling Lucky, button.”**  
     - Press **Enter** to go directly to the first result without seeing the list.  

---

✅ **Summary:**  
- **Google Search** → shows the full list of results.  
- **I’m Feeling Lucky** → jumps straight to the first result.
