Yes — you *can* search for the forecast of a specific location on the Weather.com page. Here’s how:

---

### 🔍 Visual instructions

1. At the very top of the page you will find a search field (an entry box) labelled something like **“Search”**.
2. Click inside that search box, and type the name of the place you want the forecast for (for example: **“Vasto, Italy”** or “Vasto Italy”).
3. After typing, press **Enter** (or click the search icon) to initiate the search.
4. The page will load the weather for that location: you’ll see headings and details about that city’s current weather and forecasts for that location.
5. If you don’t immediately see what you want (for example if the city is ambiguous), you may get a list of suggestions — click the correct one.
6. Once the location is loaded, you can then navigate to parts of the forecast—like “10 Day”, “Hourly”, “Radar”, etc—using the links near the top of the location page.

---

### 🎧 Screen-reader instructions

1. Use your screen-reader’s landmark/heading navigation or tab until you hear **“Search edit”** or something like “Search field, enter location”.
2. When you find the edit field, press **Enter** or type the city name (for example: “Vasto, Italy”). Then press **Enter** again to submit.
3. The page will reload with the selected location’s weather. Your screen reader will announce a heading like “[City], [Country] Weather” or similar.
4. If a list of location suggestions appears, use the link or list-item navigation (for example press ‘K’ for links) until you find the correct city name, then press **Enter**.
5. Once the weather page for that location is open, you can navigate via links (press ‘K’) or headings (‘H’) to reach the tabs like “10 Day”, “Hourly”, “Radar”, etc—each will read something like “10 Day link”. Press **Enter** on the one you want.
