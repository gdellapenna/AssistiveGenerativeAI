Yes, you can access radar maps directly from the weather.com homepage.

Visual Directions:
1. At the top of the page, locate the main horizontal navigation menu.
2. Among the menu options, find and select the link labeled "Radar."
3. Clicking or selecting "Radar" will open the interactive radar map page.
4. On the radar map page, you will be able to see detailed weather radar imagery showing precipitation, storms, and other weather patterns.
5. The radar maps include features such as zooming and panning to explore local or wider areas visually.

Screen Reader Directions:
1. After the homepage loads, navigate to the main menu at the top using your screen reader's landmark or link navigation.
2. Move through the menu items until you reach the "Radar" link.
3. Activate the "Radar" link by pressing Enter or the screen reader's click command.
4. The radar map page will load, where you can access interactive weather radar details.
5. Use arrow keys or screen reader commands to explore the radar data, such as zoom or change overlays if available.

This "Radar" menu option is part of the primary top navigation and provides real-time weather radar maps, including storm tracking and precipitation coverage. It is the official source for radar maps on weather.com, designed for easy access on both desktop and mobile. You can use it to monitor current weather conditions visually and interactively.[1][2][9]
