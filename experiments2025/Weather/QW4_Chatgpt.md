### 🔍 Visual instructions

2. Click or tap the **“Radar”** link.
3. After that, you’ll land on a page with interactive map content: e.g., “Interactive Weather Map” or “US Satellite & Radar” with map layers. (See examples of map pages on the site.) ([The Weather Channel][1])

### 🎧 Screen-reader instructions

1. Use your screen reader to jump to the top of the page (Landmark: Banner or Navigation). You’ll hear something like “Search edit” then “Menu button” then a list of links.
2. Use the Link navigation (for example press ‘K’ in JAWS/NVDA) until you hear a link labelled **“Radar”**.
3. Press **Enter** on the “Radar” link.
4. You will arrive on a page where you’ll hear a heading such as “Interactive weather map” or “US Satellite & Radar Map”.
5. On this page listen for a map region; the screen reader may announce it as “Map” or “Interactive map” and you can arrow-through or navigate to controls for zoom in/out and layer options.
6. If you want the radar for your specific city, first go to the “Search edit” field (you can use Landmark or Tab until “Search edit text”) and type your city (e.g., “Vasto, Italy”). Press Enter and select the appropriate suggestion. Then once the city’s weather page loads, navigate via links to the “Radar” section on that page.

   * You’ll hear: “Radar link” – use the Link navigation to find it and press Enter.
   * Then you will hear the interactive map and can navigate controls as before.
