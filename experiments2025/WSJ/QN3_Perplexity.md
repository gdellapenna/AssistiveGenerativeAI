## Main Navigation Sections
The Wall Street Journal homepage prominently displays main sections in the top navigation menu, including Politics, Economics, Markets, World, and Life & Arts. These categories organize access to specialized news content across the site.[1][11]

## Featured Content Sections
- **Breaking News**: Top hero area with lead headlines on current events like AI developments and business deals.
- **Markets**: Dedicated area for financial updates, stock movements, and economic indicators.
- **U.S. & World**: Stories on domestic and international news, including politics and global economy.
- **Opinion**: Editorials, columns, and reviews on policy, culture, and debates.[2][3][1]

## Additional Page Sections
- **Business & Finance**: Coverage of corporate news, tariffs, and investments.
- **Multimedia/Video**: Sidebar widgets for videos and popular stories.
- **Print Edition Links**: References to Front Page, U.S. News, Life & Arts, and Journal Report.[1][2]

Screen readers announce these as heading levels (H2/H3) or list items in the main nav landmark; use heading navigation to jump between them.[1]
