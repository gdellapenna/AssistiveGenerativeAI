## 🗂 Main sections (on WSJ.com)

When you browse WSJ’s website, you will typically see these main sections (or “tabs/menus”):

* **Business** — coverage of companies, industries, management, and general business-related news. ([Guida Newman][2])
* **Markets** — financial markets, stock markets, bonds, commodities, currencies, and related market-data and analysis. ([Guida Newman][2])
* **World** — international news: politics, global affairs, diplomacy, regional news coverage by continent or region. ([Guida Newman][2])
* **Opinion** — editorials, commentaries, opinions, columns. ([Wikipedia][3])
* **Technology (Tech)** — coverage of technology, innovation, companies in tech, possibly cybersecurity, “Future of Everything” etc. ([Dow Jones Images][1])
* **Life & Arts** — culture, lifestyle, arts, travel, design, personal-finance advice, broader societal / lifestyle pieces. ([Wall Street Journal][4])

In addition, there are **supplemental or specialized sections** (sometimes part of the regular site structure, sometimes occasional/supplemental), such as:

* **Real Estate / Mansion** — high-end real-estate and property market coverage. ([Index of /][5])
* **“Magazine” / Feature-style content** — more in-depth features, long-form stories, lifestyle and luxury-oriented content. On print this corresponds to the “WSJ Magazine,” but on the web similar content is grouped under feature/lifestyle sections. ([Index of /][5])

## 🎛 Additional navigation/tools beyond main news sections

Beyond the content sections, WSJ.com offers auxiliary navigation and tools:

* A **search box** (usually top-right) — to search for company names, keywords or topics. ([Dow Jones Images][1])
* A **“Latest Headlines” / “Breaking News” feed** — useful to catch recent developments quickly, across all sections. ([Dow Jones Images][1])
* A **“My Journal” / personalization** area — for subscribed or logged-in users to save articles, manage preferences, follow topics. ([Pitt][6])
