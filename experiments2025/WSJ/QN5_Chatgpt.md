Here’s **how you can search for a specific news article** on The Wall Street Journal (WSJ.com), both in a **visual / sighted-browser** sense and in a **screen-reader / keyboard-navigation** sense. I rely on what the site documentation says about its search features. ([Guida Newman][1])

---

## 🔍 How to search on WSJ.com — Visual directions

1. Go to the WSJ homepage. At the top-right corner, look for a **search box** (usually a small text field with a magnifying-glass icon). According to the site’s documentation, this is where you enter keywords, company names or phrases. ([Dow Jones Images][2])
2. Click on that search box, type the word(s) or phrase you want to search for (for example, a person’s name, company, or topic).
3. If available — for more precise results — look for an **“Advanced Search”** option (often a link near the search field). This lets you refine the search: you can limit by subject (e.g. Business, World), region, date or time range. ([Guida Newman][1])
4. Press Enter or click on the search button (magnifying-glass). The site will then show results matching your query. These may include recent articles, archived content (last few years), sometimes print-edition archives. ([Guida Newman][1])
5. From the list of search results, click on a title that matches what you want — that opens the full article page.

---

## 🎧 How to search on WSJ.com using a screen-reader / keyboard

1. Right after the page loads, use your screen-reader command to jump to the **search box**. Depending on your screen reader, you might press “Ctrl + F” or “find” for “search,” or navigate by landmarks / regions until you hear something like “Search – edit text” or “Search box.”
2. Once the search box is focused, type your search keywords (e.g. “climate change”, or “Tesla”), then press Enter (or the equivalent key to submit the form).
3. If there is an “Advanced Search” link, navigate to it (e.g. by pressing “Tab” repeatedly until you hear “Advanced Search link”), then press Enter — then fill in additional fields (e.g. date range, subject, region) and submit.
4. After submission, your screen reader will land on the list of results. Use the command to move through links (e.g. “next link”) until you hear an article title that seems relevant.
5. When you find the correct title, press Enter (or “activate link”) to open it. Your screen reader will then read the article page (or at least its accessible portion), including headline, summary or full text (if accessible).

---

## ⚠️ What to know before searching: paywall and access limitations

* WSJ.com offers a **searchable archive** of articles for up to a few years. ([Guida Newman][1])
* However — many articles may be behind a **paywall** or require a subscription/login to read the full content. ([biblioteca.luiss.it][3])
* So even if you successfully find an article via search, you might find only the headline and teaser, or a “sign-in” prompt if you don’t have active subscription.
