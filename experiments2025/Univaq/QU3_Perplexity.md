Yes, the University of L’Aquila website has menus organized primarily in a top navigation bar. The main menu options on the homepage include:

- Home: The central landing page of the site.
- University: Information about the university’s overall structure and details.
- Departments: Lists the various academic departments such as Information Engineering, Computer Science and Mathematics, Civil Engineering, Physical and Chemical Sciences, and others.
- Courses: Details about degree programs and academic courses offered.
- Research: Information about ongoing research activities, projects, and centers.
- International: Resources for international students, study abroad, and collaborations.
- Student Services: Services directed at students like enrollment, administrative support, and welfare.
- Contacts: Contact details for various university offices and departments.

These menus typically open submenus or pages with more specific links related to each category. For example, under "Departments," you might find a list of the seven main departments.

How to access the menus with a screen reader on the homepage:

- After the page loads, press **Tab** to reach the top navigation menu.
- The screen reader reads out each menu item in sequence: "Home," "University," "Departments," "Courses," "Research," "International," "Student Services," and "Contacts."
- Use arrow keys (right or down) to move between menu items.
- Press **Enter** to open a specific menu or submenu.
- To navigate submenu items, use arrow keys or Tab again and activate linked pages with **Enter**.
- Use standard screen reader navigation commands like 'H' to jump to headings if menus have headers or 'Tab' to move through links.
