Yes — the **current homepage of Università degli Studi dell’Aquila** does include accessibility features.  

---

## 🖥️ Visual navigation (seeing the features)

- At the **top utility bar** (the very top of the page), you’ll find:  
  - **Font size selector**: three “A” letters in different sizes. Clicking on them increases or decreases the text size.  
  - **High contrast option**: a button labeled **“Alto contrasto”** (High contrast). Clicking it switches the page to a high-contrast version for better readability.  

These controls are small icons/links aligned horizontally next to the *EN (English)* and *Login* links.

---

## 🎧 Screen reader navigation (hearing the features)

- Navigate to the **top of the page** using the Tab key or by moving through links.  
- The screen reader will announce:  
  - “A link” (small font size), “A link” (medium font size), “A link” (large font size).  
  - Then “Alto contrasto link.”  
- Press **Enter** on one of the “A” links to change the font size.  
- Press **Enter** on “Alto contrasto” to activate the high-contrast version.  

---

✅ So yes, the homepage provides **both a font size selector and a high contrast mode** as accessibility features.
