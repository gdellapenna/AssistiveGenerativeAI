This is the initial analysis of the home page for the Università degli Studi dell'Aquila (`https://www.univaq.it`).

The page is primarily in **Italian**, which is an important consideration for an English-speaking user. However, there is a clear English language option available.

***

### Initial Page Overview and Language Selection

When the page loads, the first thing visible is the main title and several utility links, followed by the main navigation.

#### 1. Change Language to English

Since the page is in Italian, the first step is to switch to English.

**Visual Directions:**
Look for a small section near the top right of the page that contains the text **"| EN | Login |"**. Click on the link that says **"EN"**.

**Screen Reader Directions:**
Use your screen reader's navigation commands to find a **link** labeled **"EN"** or the **link** immediately following the utility links at the top of the page. Activate this link.

***

### 2. Main Navigation Structure (Post-Language Selection)

The page offers two main methods of navigation: a set of **"Quick Access"** links at the very top, and a primary horizontal menu. There is also a dedicated menu for selecting a specific user **"Profile"**.

#### A. Quick Access Links

Near the top of the page, there are several utility links (most likely still in Italian before the language switch).

**Screen Reader Directions:**
Look for the area with the heading or section labeled **"Accesso veloce"** (Quick Access). Common links include:
* **Dipartimenti** (Departments)
* **Segreterie studenti** (Student Offices)
* **Orientarsi alla scelta del corso** (Guidance for Course Selection)
* **Sistema bibliotecario di Ateneo (SBA)** (University Library System)

#### B. Profile Selection Menu

Just below the main logo, there is a key area for navigating based on your role, labeled **"Scegli il tuo profilo"** (Choose your profile). This is critical for finding relevant information.

**Visual Directions:**
Look for a drop-down or a set of buttons/links labeled **"Scegli il tuo profilo"**. The options are:
* **Futura matricola** (Future student/applicant)
* **Studente** (Current student)
* **Studente internazionale** (International student)
* **Laureata e laureato** (Graduate)
* **Personale** (Staff)
* **Imprese** (Companies)

**Screen Reader Directions:**
Look for the **Heading (H2 or H3)** or section labeled **"Scegli il tuo profilo"**. Then, navigate to the list of **links** immediately following it. Find and activate the link corresponding to your role, such as **"Studente internazionale"**.

#### C. Primary Site Menu

The main navigation bar is horizontal and contains the major sections of the university:

**Visual Directions:**
The main links are large and clearly visible below the profile section:
1.  **Ateneo** (University/Institution)
2.  **Didattica** (Education/Teaching)
3.  **Ricerca** (Research)
4.  **Internazionalità** (International)

**Screen Reader Directions:**
Navigate by **Heading (H2)** or by looking for the main **navigation links** (usually grouped in a list) that are labeled: **"Ateneo"**, **"Didattica"**, **"Ricerca"**, and **"Internazionalità"**.

***

### 3. Interactive Elements on the Page

There are two major interactive elements you can use to search for information:

#### A. The Search Box

**Visual Directions:**
Near the top right, above the main navigation, there is an input field labeled **"cerca"** (search) next to a search icon. You can type your search terms here.

**Screen Reader Directions:**
Look for the **Search Form** or the **input field** labeled **"cerca"**. You can type your query into this edit field and then activate the adjacent **button** to submit your search.

#### B. Cookie Consent Banner

At the bottom of the page, there is a **Cookie Consent Banner** that you must interact with.

**Visual Directions:**
A large banner covers the bottom of the screen. It has three main buttons:
1.  **"Accetta tutto"** (Accept all)
2.  **"Accetta necessari"** (Accept necessary)
3.  **"Rifiuta cookie non necessari"** (Refuse non-necessary cookies)

**Screen Reader Directions:**
This is usually the **last interactive element** on the page. Use the "tab" key or link navigation commands to find one of the three buttons: **"Accetta tutto"**, **"Accetta necessari"**, or **"Rifiuta cookie non necessari"**. Select the option you prefer to dismiss the banner.
