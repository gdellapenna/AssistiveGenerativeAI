### Visible Content and Layout
- The homepage features a sleek, minimalist design with prominent categories such as **Hotels, Things To Do, Restaurants, Flights, Vacation Rentals**, and a **Search All** option, which are the main interactive categories.[2]
- Each category links to dedicated pages with filters, suggestions, and curated content, enhancing user navigation.[2]
- The homepage has sections for travel inspiration, popular destinations, community engagement (forums, reviews, travel stories), and special features like a trip builder.[2]

### Interactive Elements
- The search bar, divided into categories, is the main interactive tool and is often sticky, meaning it stays accessible as you scroll, facilitating easy access.[2]
- Filters and options for flights, hotels, and attractions allow input of details such as dates, locations, and preferences.
- Community features include discussion forums, review submissions, and storytelling, all designed to encourage interaction.[2]

### Navigation Tips
- The menu and filters are located at the top and within specific pages, often with sticky behavior for ease of use.
- Clicking on a category like "Hotels" or "Flights" will take you to sub-pages with more refined search options.
- To explore individual reviews, travel stories, or forums, navigate through dedicated links typically found in the footer or main menu.

### How to Use a Screen Reader
- Screen readers will announce the main navigation categories at the top of each page, such as "Hotels, Things To Do, Restaurants," etc.
- Focus moves to the search bar, which is usually labeled clearly (e.g., "search all" or specific categories).
- Use arrow keys or tab to navigate through filters, options, and links.
- Special features like forum discussions and reviews are usually announced with labels like "discussions," "reviews," or "write a review."
- Content sections such as travel inspiration are organized in headings or clear titles, which can be navigated via heading shortcuts.
