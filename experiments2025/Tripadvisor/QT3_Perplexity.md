The Tripadvisor homepage includes several menu options designed to help users navigate the site efficiently. Here are the main menus and their options:

1. **Top Navigation Menu (Main Menu)**
   - Hotels
   - Things to Do
   - Restaurants
   - Flights
   - Vacation Rentals
   - Search All (a catch-all search menu for everything on the site)

2. **Search Filters and Submenus**
   - When selecting a category like Flights or Hotels, submenus appear with specific filters. For example, Flights allow you to enter departure, destination, dates, and preferences.
   - Hotels and Things to Do have options to filter by location, traveler ratings, price range, amenities, and more.

3. **Community and Additional Features Menu**
   - Forum (for traveler discussions)
   - Travel Stories (blogs and user experiences)
   - Reviews (leave or read reviews)
   - Add a Place or Write a Review

4. **Footer Menu (usually at the bottom)**
   - About Us
   - Contact
   - Privacy Policy
   - Terms and Conditions
   - Tripadvisor Foundation and Donation Links

### Using a Screen Reader:
- The main horizontal menu at the top is announced as navigation landmarks with each category named (e.g., "Hotels," "Flights," "Restaurants").
- Use Tab or arrow keys to move through the menu items; each item is a link or button.
- Selecting a main menu item loads a new page or opens a submenu with filters and detailed options.
- Additional menus like Forum, Travel Stories, and Reviews are typically found either in a secondary navigation area or footer, announced as separate navigation landmarks, helping you locate community features.
- Footer links are accessible by tabbing through page footer navigation and described usually as legal or informational links.

This site organizes content with a clear hierarchy: broad categories in the main menu, refined filters and options after selection, and community support navigation in secondary menus.[1][2]
