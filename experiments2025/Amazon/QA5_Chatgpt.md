Yes — you *can* search for a specific book on the Amazon homepage. Here’s how, with both a **visual** step-by-step and a **screen-reader** version.

---

### Visual Instructions

1. At the top of the homepage, locate the search bar (an input field) with placeholder text such as “Search Amazon” (or similar).
2. Click in the search field and type the name of the book you’re looking for (for example: *“The Hitchhiker’s Guide to the Galaxy”*).
3. Optionally, just to the left of the search input, there may be a drop-down labelled “All” (or “All Departments”) — click that and choose **Books** to narrow the search to the Books department.
4. After entering your book title (and selecting Books, if you did that), click the Search button (often a magnifying glass icon) on the right of the search field.
5. The results page will load showing books matching your search term. If you want a specific edition, you might need to scroll through the results or apply filters (format, language, publication date) to find the exact one.
6. If the book doesn’t show up immediately, you can try using more specific keywords (author’s name, ISBN) or the “Advanced Search” for Books department (see below).
7. For a very precise search: scroll to the bottom of the Books department page (or use the URL) to find **Advanced Search: Books** — where you can enter Title, Author, ISBN, Publisher, etc. ([Amazon][1])

---

### Screen-Reader Instructions

1. From the homepage, press `Ctrl + Home` to go to the top of the page (on Windows) or use your screen-reader’s “go to beginning” command.
2. Use the Tab key (or your screen-reader’s “next focusable element” command) until you hear something like:

   * “Search Amazon edit” (this is the input field)
   * Just before that you may hear “All Departments drop-down button” (if available).
3. If you hear “All Departments drop-down button”, press `Enter` or `Space` to expand it. Use the arrow keys (or Tab) to navigate to “Books” and press `Enter` to select it. If you don’t hear that drop-down, you can skip this and search across all departments (but choosing “Books” narrows your results).
4. Once you hear “Search Amazon edit”, type the book title (for example: *“The Hitchhiker’s Guide to the Galaxy”*).
5. Press `Tab` until you reach “Search button” (or something like “Go” or the magnifying-glass icon button). Press `Enter` to submit the search.
6. The results page will load. Use your screen reader’s headings (`H`) or link list (`Insert+F7` in NVDA) or list of search results commands to locate the result items. Each item will usually be announced with the book title, format (hardcover/paperback/Kindle), price, and “Add to Cart” or “See more formats”.
7. If you want to refine further (for example by ISBN or specific edition), you can navigate (via Tab or heading H) to a link labelled “Advanced Search: Books” (or similar) — activate it. On that page you’ll hear fields like “Title edit”, “Author edit”, “ISBN edit”, etc. Enter your parameters and then find the “Go” or “Search” button and press `Enter`.

   * Note: The advanced search page is documented on Amazon’s help system. ([Amazon][1])
