Yes — on the Amazon homepage you *can* restrict your search to books only. Here’s **how** to do it, in both **visual** steps and **screen-reader** steps.

---

### Visual Instructions

1. Near the top of the page locate the search bar: a drop-down (often labelled “All” or “All Departments”) immediately to the left of the text input field.
2. Click that drop-down menu. A list of product departments will appear (e.g., “Books”, “Electronics”, “Clothing”, etc.).
3. From that list select **Books** (or “Books & eBooks” depending on the locale).
4. With “Books” selected as the department, click into the search input field and type your book title, author, ISBN, etc.
5. Then click the Search (magnifying-glass) button.
6. The results you get will be constrained to the Books category.

Additionally, if you’d like more precision (for example by author, ISBN, format) you can find the “Advanced Search” option for Books in Amazon. ([MarketAspex -][1])

---

### Screen-Reader Instructions

1. Press `Ctrl + Home` (or your screen reader’s “go to top” command) to reach the top of the page.
2. Use the Tab key (or “next link/control” command) until you hear something like **“All departments drop-down button”**.
3. Press `Enter` or `Space` to open the drop-down.
4. Use the arrow keys (or continue Tab) until you hear **“Books link”** (or “Books & eBooks link”). Press `Enter` to select it.
5. Next you will hear “Search Amazon edit” (this is the search input field). Type the name of the book you want.
6. Then press `Tab` until you hear “Search button” (or similar). Press `Enter`.
7. The resulting page will list only books matching your search.
